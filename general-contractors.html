General Contractor (GC)

"A General Contractor is the main contractor hired by the project owner (client) to oversee the entire construction project. They are responsible for the project's execution, from planning and organizing to the final completion. The GC typically serves as the primary point of contact for the client and coordinates all the activities on the job site. "

Project Management

" The GC is responsible for managing the entire construction project. This includes scheduling, budgeting, overseeing the work, and ensuring everything is completed on time and within budget. "

Hiring Subcontractors

"The general contractor hires subcontractors to perform specific tasks (like plumbing, electrical work, or drywall installation). They ensure that all subcontractors are licensed, insured, and qualified to do the work."

Obtaining Permits

"The GC typically handles all the paperwork needed to obtain the required building permits and inspections. "

Procurement of Materials

"The GC often manages the procurement of materials and supplies required for the project, ensuring that everything is delivered on time. "

Quality Control

"Ensuring that the work meets the specified standards and that the finished product is safe, functional, and up to code. "

Coordination and Communication

"The GC is the central point for all communication on a construction project. They coordinate between the client, subcontractors, suppliers, and inspectors. "
